    Okinawa de Suki ni Natta Ko ga Hougen Sugite Tsurasugiru

    Okitsura: Fell in Love with an Okinawan Girl, but I Just Wish I Know What She's Saying
    • Studio: Millipensee
    • Season: Winter
    • PV: https://youtu.be/o5WMDPjBZVw
    • Why I liked it:

      By using a familiar romcom forumula, Okitsura educates the viewer on Okinawan history and culture. I really enjoyed learning about Okinawan culture alongside Teruaki, the protagonist. Millipensee uses a lot of animation techniques that remind me of cel animation. It's a very intentional look that even give off an "Okinawa"-ish impression. It has a light and breezy flow. It's clear they see animation as an art form! The series' concept wouldn't work if the characters weren't good. I really enjoyed getting to spend time with Kyan-san, Higa-san, and Agena-san. The character moe was very strong. Even the old grandmas were moe!

    mono

    • Studio: Soigne
    • Season: Spring
    • PV: https://youtu.be/TZn2ZUkoJrU, https://youtu.be/jxdHEsXEW4Y
    • Why I liked it:

      Another series from YuruCamp's creator, あfろ! It's a colourful and lively slice of life about recovering from a slump and finding new motivation! It's a tour of the parts of Yamanashi prefecture that don't involve camping. Growth, adventure, and love... I really loved the time I spent with the characters in this show, I even rewatched each episode at least once! With a colourful style and excellent voice acting the show really came to life and stole my heart! Kiriyama An... I will die for you... To ensure you get to smile for another day... The story is simple, but really poignant!

    Ninja to Koroshiya no Futarigurashi

    A Ninja and an Assassin Under One Roof
    • Studio: SHAFT
    • Season: Spring
    • PV: https://youtu.be/Eud4h_Bt3Sc
    • Why I liked it:

      After Monogatari Off & Monster Season I was feeling optimistic about SHAFT's future. It doesn't reach the levels of Hidamari Sketch or Madoka, but NinKoro is one of my favourite SHAFT works! The characters are full of personality, the style's a little offbeat, the directing is high quality. It's a SHAFT anime! What else can I say? SHAFT is back!?

    Food Court de, Mata Ashita.

    See You Tomorrow at the Food Court
    • Studio: Atelier Pontdarc
    • Season: Summer
    • PV: https://youtu.be/caqJxV4p-KI
    • Why I liked it:

      Two girls meet at the mall's food court after school. They chat and joke around, share their troubles with each other and cause trouble for each other. The simple concept let's the series focus on non-stop comedy. It's one of the funniest anime I've ever watched. It only ran for 6 episodes but they're jam-packed and guaranteed to make you smile.

    Ruri no Houseki

    Ruri Rocks
    • Studio: Studio Bind
    • Season: Summer
    • PV: https://youtu.be/ksfH7W7N9w4, https://youtu.be/kdh0ucpJEH8
    • Why I liked it:

      The natural world is beautiful, and bigger than we can imagine. Much bigger than us. Everything in nature is connected by a process of creation that's still ongoing. Rivers and mountains are created over the course of millions of years. Ruri no Houseki is a story about discovering this process that's hidden in plain sight. Look down at your feet and you might discover evidence of this never ending cycle. By utilizing moe, Ruri no Houseki turns a monotonous lecture into an exciting and poignant story. Watching over Ruri's growth as her world widens up with the help of Nagi was very inspiring. We all have the same potential for curiosity and growth as Ruri.

    Necronomico no Cosmic Horror Show

    Necronomico and the Cosmic Horror Show
    • Studio: Studio Gokumi
    • Season: Summer
    • PV: https://youtu.be/98tanj-P94Y
    • Why I liked it:

      This is an original anime! It's a thrilling adventure about fighting against unfair odds, overcoming adversity, and doing anything for the person you love! It features moe interpretations of classic cosmic horror monsters which gives it a memorable style. There's a lot of struggling and suffering in this show, it's a lot of fun!

    Tensei Akujo no Kuro Rekishi

    The Dark History of the Reincarnated Villainess
    • Studio: Studio Deen
    • Season: Fall
    • PV: https://youtu.be/gKmcRxitVxg
    • Why I liked it:

      I wasn't expecting too much from this show, but I was interested in the unique take on the concept enough to give it a shot. I was surprised while watching the first episode. It has a manic pace, and felt like it was really leaning into the satire. "Is this done by the Di Gi Charat director or something?". Oh, it is! The show caught me off guard a second time, I'm attached to the main character! She develops an interesting complex where she refuses to accept that people trust her - surely they're constantly waiting for her to reveal her evil nature at which point she'd be killed. This pushes her to make a bunch of bad decisions that put her in grave danger. She's an interesting character!

    Shuumatsu Touring

    Touring After the Apocalypse
    • Studio: Nexus
    • Season: Fall
    • PV: https://youtu.be/-dC7gzY9pa4
    • Why I liked it:

      The world is in ruins and two cute girls explore it on their Serow - a Yamaha road bike. It's about life and spirit. Seeings these girls innocently explore a destroyed world fills my heart with hope. The world is a beautiful place.

      "Do you remember that day? The sky was higher than we'd ever imagined, the breeze felt so good... and the sun was just so dazzling... and the air was full of all kinds of scents. After leaving the shelter... we had fun times, first-ever experiences, things that didn't work out. A lot of stuff happened. What I want to see now isn't the outside world that I've always dreamed of, but this world right now, I think."

    Towa no Yuugure

    Dusk Beyond the End of the World
    • Studio: P.A. Works
    • Season: Fall
    • PV: https://youtu.be/iSzAu-ou44Y, https://youtu.be/f02Yho-c-Pw
    • Why I liked it:

      Another original anime! This one isn't amazing... but I really appreciate it's aesthetics and themes. It feels like an anime from the heisei era - it's character designs and world don't follow modern trends. It feels otaku-like, almost like it could have been a VN. These aesthetics aren't an accident. The show is about a conflict between Heisei and Reiwa ideals! It's really refreshing to watch an anime that handles topics like love and gender in such a forwarding thinking way. I really fell in love with the characters. I was crying by the end, it was really touching!
